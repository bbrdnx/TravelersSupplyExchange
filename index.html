Find and get gear nearby, today

  1. Home feed. Seeded Waikiki inventory, closest first. Supply already exists near you. The problem is finding it, not buying more. Home →
  2. Search. Type a need and the category auto-detects, then results filter. Fastest path to "what can I actually get right now." Results →
  3. Item detail. Photo gallery, plain one-line description, circulation count. You see exactly what you're picking up before you commit. Detail →
  4. Trust, designed in. Item origin and owner history, verified badge, ratings, not-for-sale banner. Trust between strangers is the product, not fine print. Origin →
  5. Safe exchange. Public meetup, no money, bring a friend. Safety gets its own screen instead of a buried disclaimer. Exchange →
  6. Messages. DMs tied to the specific item. Coordination stays in context with what's being exchanged. Messages →
  7. Profiles. Exchanges, ratings, current listings. Reputation is what makes a stranger handoff feel safe. Profile →
Riskiest assumption the MVP tests: a traveler can find and safely get gear nearby, right now. Inventory is seeded for the demo.

Leave useful gear behind, not in the trash

  1. Entry point. "List gear you can't take home" lives right on the home feed. Meets the traveler at the leaving-soon moment, when the gear becomes a burden. Home →
  2. Details + auto-category. The name drives category detection; condition sets quality. Less typing means more gear actually gets listed. Details →
  3. Photo nudge. Optional, but a note explains listings with photos get requested 3× more. Encourages trust without forcing friction. Photo →
  4. Availability windows. Pick days and time ranges, not a single departure date. Matches how travelers actually plan a handoff. Logistics →
  5. Preview parity. The listing card is identical to the Results view. What you publish is exactly what the next traveler sees. Preview →
  6. Live. Confirmation, with the item's first circulation teased. Closes the supply side of the loop. Done →
Flow 2 supplies the inventory Flow 1 depends on. Tap any step to jump to that screen.

Keep the loop going

  1. Return prompt. The borrowed item sits on home with a badge teaser. The return is prompted, so gear doesn't quietly leave the system. Borrowed card →
  2. Pass it on. A sheet offers relisting, or asks why if you can't. Graceful exits when an item is consumed or worn out, not just dead ends. Open sheet →
  3. Prefilled relist. Same listing flow, every field filled from the item you borrowed. Passing on is easier than the first listing ever was. Relist →
  4. Achievement. Finishing unlocks the Trail Keeper badge with an animation, then resets. The rewarded behavior is circulation, the hardest thing to get people to do. See badge →
This is the loop-closing behavior, the riskiest and most novel part of the concept. It turns a one-time borrow into ongoing circulation.
